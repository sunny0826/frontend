# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

OpenShare serves open source developers, enterprises, organizations, and open source communities.

Developers use it to make contribution history, capability signals, rewards, messages, and profile information visible and manageable across platforms. Enterprises and organizations use it to discover developer value, reach relevant talent, allocate contribution incentives, and manage organization-level activity. Communities use it to understand ecosystem health, identify contributors, and support sustained participation.

## Product Purpose

OpenShare connects cross-platform open source activity, developer capability profiles, organization demand, and points incentives into a verifiable value network.

Success means a visitor can quickly understand why contribution value matters, then move into concrete product workflows: searching repositories or developers, reading insight data, managing points, handling rewards, configuring accounts, and operating organizations.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, precise, and motivating.

The interface should feel credible enough for data-backed talent decisions, specific enough for open source contributors to recognize their work, and energetic enough to make contribution rewards feel tangible.

## Anti-references

OpenShare should not look like a traditional recruiting website, a cryptocurrency product, or an overdone cyber aesthetic.

Avoid generic job-board patterns, speculative token-economy visual language, heavy neon effects, decorative terminal tropes, and visual treatments that make the platform feel less credible than the contribution data it presents.

## Design Principles

Lead with contribution value. The brand surface should make open source work feel concrete, measurable, and worth returning value to.

Earn trust before asking for action. Data claims, product navigation, and reward mechanics should feel transparent and verifiable.

Connect the ecosystem, not just the user. Developers, organizations, and communities should read as parts of one value network.

Keep momentum visible. The experience should make activity, ranking, outreach, rewards, and account state feel current rather than static.

Respect bilingual comprehension. English and Chinese copy should be readable, direct, and layout-safe without relying on one language as the design default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain readable contrast in both dark and light themes, support keyboard navigation and visible focus states, preserve reduced-motion alternatives, and keep Chinese and English text legible across responsive layouts.
